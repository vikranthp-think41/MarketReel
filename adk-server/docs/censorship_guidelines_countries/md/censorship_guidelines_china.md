MarketReel — Global Film Distribution Intelligence ChinaFilm Censorship & Certification GuidelinesNational Radio and Television Administration (NRTA) & Film Bureau — Regulatory Intelligence1. Regulatory AuthorityThe National Radio and Television Administration (NRTA), formerly SARFT, administers all film content regulation in China. The Film Bureau (电影局) under NRTA handles theatrical film approval. China operates a binary approval system — films are either approved (批准) or rejected (不批准). There is no tiered age-rating system for theatrical releases. All approved films are considered suitable for general audiences, meaning content appropriate only for adults cannot legally receive theatrical release in its unedited form.Governing Law: Film Industry Promotion Law of the People's Republic of China (2017). Administrative authority: NRTA and the CCP Propaganda Department.2. Absolutely Prohibited Content — Automatic RejectionAny content challenging the authority, legitimacy, or territorial claims of the Chinese Communist Party (CCP) or the People's Republic of China government.Any depiction or positive reference to Taiwan as an independent nation-state. Taiwan must be shown as part of China or not referenced geographically at all.Any depiction or positive reference to Tibet as independent, or to the Dalai Lama as a legitimate political or spiritual leader.Any content relating to the Tiananmen Square incident of 1989 — whether documentary, fictional, or allegorical.Xinjiang content depicting Uyghur persecution, detention camps, or Chinese government policy in the region.Falun Gong depicted positively or neutrally as a legitimate spiritual practice.Homosexual content — LGBTQ relationships, same-sex romance, or any positive portrayal of non-heterosexual identity, whether explicit or implied.Graphic violence, gore, or scenes glorifying criminal behaviour.Foreign films that portray China, Chinese people, or Chinese culture negatively, stereotypically, or disrespectfully.Supernatural, ghost, and reincarnation content — enforcement varies; framing as superstition rather than science increases risk. Sci-fi time travel with scientific framing is generally acceptable.Content that undermines Chinese national unity or social stability.4. Import Quota SystemRevenue-Sharing Films: 34 films per year. Foreign distributor receives approximately 25% of box office gross.Flat-Fee Films: Unlimited quantity; fixed fee paid regardless of box office. Lower financial upside but no quota constraint.Blackout Periods: China enforces domestic film protection periods (Chinese New Year, National Day Golden Week). Foreign films blocked during these windows.Co-productions: Films co-produced with approved Chinese studios bypass the quota system entirely. Minimum 1/3 Chinese cast, 1/3 production in China, and script pre-approval required.AGENT QUOTA RULERevenue-sharing slots are highly competitive. Submit applications 6–9 months before intended release. If quota is full, flat-fee release or delay to next year are the only options.6. Submission ProcessEngage an approved China distribution partner licensed by the Film Bureau.Submit script and synopsis for preliminary review before production (strongly recommended).Submit completed film with Chinese subtitles and full technical materials to Film Bureau.Review period: 30–90 days (no statutory limit; can extend significantly for sensitive content).If modifications required, execute changes and resubmit for confirmation.Upon final approval, Film Bureau issues Dragon Seal (龙标) — mandatory on-screen display before film plays.7.
