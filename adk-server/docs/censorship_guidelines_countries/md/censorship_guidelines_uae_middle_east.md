Regulatory AuthoritiesThe UAE Media Regulatory Office (MRO), formerly the National Media Council (NMC), is the primary content regulator for the UAE. It is the most liberalised regulatory body in the Gulf region. This is both a censorship ground and a criminal offence under UAE Penal Code.Any content that insults UAE ruling family (Al Nahyan), Saudi royal family, or other Gulf ruling dynasties.LGBTQ content — any depiction, positive framing, or normalisation of same-sex relationships, gender non-conforming identity, or homosexuality. Consensual same-sex acts are criminal in UAE (up to 10 years imprisonment). Films with any LGBTQ content are routinely cut or banned.Explicit sexual content including nudity, sexual acts, or heavily suggestive sexual behaviour.Content promoting or glorifying drug or alcohol consumption.Content depicting or glorifying terrorism or Sunni-Shia sectarian violence.Content promoting atheism or apostasy from Islam.Content depicting Israel positively or Zionism sympathetically. Note: UAE Abraham Accords 2020 have somewhat shifted UAE stance; Kuwait and other Gulf states remain more restrictive.4. Regional Variance NotesKuwait is consistently more restrictive than UAE — always run a separate Kuwait compliance check for films approved in Dubai.Egypt is the most commercially significant non-Gulf Arab market and operates a separate Cairo-based censorship board. Relatively more permissive on religious and political content but strict on explicit sexuality.Jordan permits relatively open cultural expression in its arthouse sector; mainstream commercial release follows Gulf-adjacent standards.Qatar post-FIFA 2022 has somewhat liberalised but core Islamic content rules remain fully in force.6. Ramadan Release WindowFilm content during Ramadan should align with Islamic values — family-appropriate, morally uplifting content.Action, horror, or hedonistic content faces heightened scrutiny and performs poorly during Ramadan.G and PG-rated family and animated films perform strongly.
