Recruited into a shadowy organization called Tenet after demonstrating his willingness to die for the mission, the Protagonist is tasked with discovering the source of this temporal inversion technology.His investigation leads to Andrei Sator, a Russian oligarch with access to technology from the future — assembled and distributed by a future algorithm designed to reverse global entropy and effectively destroy the present. Sator's estranged wife Kat is the Protagonist's entry point, a woman desperate to escape her abusive marriage.Alongside Neil — a mysterious, charming operative who seems to know far more than he reveals — the Protagonist traverses Tallinn, Mumbai, Oslo, and the Russian Arctic in a series of increasingly complex temporal set pieces. Key concept: "Inversion" is not time travel — inverted people and objects move backwards through time while interacting with the forward-moving world. The result is action sequences of unprecedented conceptual complexity: a car chase running simultaneously forwards and backwards, a building raid where two teams approach the same moment from opposite temporal directions.The climax is a military operation at Sator's epicenter in Siberia, combining a forward-moving red team and an inverted blue team attacking the same objective simultaneously across two temporal streams. Neil, it is revealed in the film's final moments, has known the Protagonist throughout his own timeline — he has been recruited by the future version of the Protagonist and is completing a loop that will end in his death. He accepts this with grace.Sator is defeated. The algorithm is not detonated.
