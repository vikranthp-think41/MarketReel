Civilization is quietly winding down. Cooper, a former NASA test pilot now farming with his family, discovers a gravitational anomaly that leads him to a hidden NASA facility run by Professor Brand.Brand reveals that a wormhole has appeared near Saturn, apparently placed there by a mysterious intelligence. Previous missions (Lazarus) have sent astronauts through to identify potentially habitable planets. Cooper is tasked with leading a follow-up mission through the wormhole, alongside Brand's daughter Amelia, physicist Romilly, and others. He leaves his son Tom and beloved daughter Murph, promising to return.The crew traverses the wormhole and visits three candidate planets. The first, Miller's planet, exists so close to a massive black hole (Gargantua) that time dilation is extreme — one hour there equals seven years on Earth. A catastrophic wave kills Doyle and wastes 23 years of relative time. The second mission to Mann's planet reveals its data was falsified by a marooned astronaut who, driven to madness by isolation, attempts to kill Cooper and hijacks their vessel.As Cooper and Amelia race to salvage the mission, Cooper's choice emerges: use the black hole's gravity to slingshot Amelia to the third planet (Edmunds') — sacrificing himself. He falls into Gargantua and enters a tesseract — a five-dimensional construct built by evolved future humans to allow communication across time. Cooper realizes he is the mysterious force that has been sending Murph messages. He transmits quantum gravity data through Morse code via a watch. Murph, now elderly, decodes it — enabling humanity's escape from Earth.Cooper exits the tesseract, is rescued near Saturn, and reunites with an aged Murph before she urges him to find Amelia.
