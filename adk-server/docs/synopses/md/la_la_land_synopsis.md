Mia, an aspiring actress working as a barista on a studio lot, and Sebastian, a jazz purist determined to open his own club, meet and clash repeatedly before falling in love.Their courtship unfolds in a series of cinematic set pieces — dancing under the stars at Griffith Observatory, wandering Hermitage-colored studio backlots, sharing the piano bench in a jazz club. Chazelle suffuses the romance with the language of classic Hollywood musicals while simultaneously deconstructing their promises.As Mia and Sebastian support each other's dreams, complications emerge. Sebastian joins a commercially successful jazz-pop band led by Keith (John Legend) to fund their life together — a compromise that Mia recognizes as a betrayal of his artistic principles. Mia writes and performs a one-woman show that fails commercially but attracts a crucial audition. They fight; she returns home to her parents in Nevada.Mia gets the call — she's won the role. But it requires moving to Paris. The relationship cannot survive the physical distance and their diverging trajectories. They part with love but no resolution.The film's final act — a wordless, eight-minute fantasy sequence — shows an alternate timeline in which Mia and Sebastian chose each other over their dreams. They have a life together, a child, happiness. Then reality reasserts itself. Years later, Mia — now a famous actress — wanders into a jazz club with her husband. The pianist is Sebastian. He looks up. She looks back. He plays the song. They share in their minds the life they didn't live. She walks out. He nods.
